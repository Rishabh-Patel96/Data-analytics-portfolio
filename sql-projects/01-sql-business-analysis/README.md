# 🚀 SQL Business Analysis Project

## 📌 Project Overview

This project is a comprehensive collection of SQL-based analyses designed to explore, segment, and evaluate business data from multiple perspectives.

The goal is to demonstrate how SQL can be used to perform **end-to-end business analysis**, including trend analysis, segmentation, performance evaluation, and reporting.

---

## 🧠 Business Objective

Modern businesses rely heavily on data to understand performance, customer behavior, and growth trends.

This project answers key analytical questions such as:

* How is the business performing over time?
* Which customers and products contribute the most value?
* How can data be segmented to derive actionable insights?
* What patterns exist across different business dimensions?

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

* SQL (Analytical SQL)
* PostgreSQL
* DataGrip (IDE)

---

## ⚙️ Key Concepts Used

* Aggregations (`SUM`, `COUNT`, `AVG`)
* Window Functions (`ROW_NUMBER`, `RANK`, `SUM OVER`)
* Cumulative Analysis
* Time-based Analysis
* Data Segmentation
* Ranking & Performance Analysis
* Part-to-Whole Analysis
* Multi-dimensional Exploration

---

## 📊 Analysis Modules

This project is structured into multiple analytical modules, each focusing on a specific business perspective:

* **Change Over Time Analysis** → Evaluating trends and growth patterns
* **Cumulative Analysis** → Understanding running totals and performance buildup
* **Customer Report Analysis** → Customer-level performance and contribution
* **Data Segmentation** → Grouping data into meaningful categories
* **Database Exploration** → Understanding dataset structure and relationships
* **Date Range Exploration** → Analyzing time windows and periods
* **Dimension Exploration** → Evaluating performance across business dimensions
* **Magnitude Analysis** → Measuring scale and distribution of metrics
* **Measure Exploration** → Deep dive into key business metrics
* **Part-to-Whole Analysis** → Understanding contribution percentages
* **Performance Analysis** → Identifying top and low performers
* **Product Report Analysis** → Product-level insights and performance
* **Ranking Analysis** → Ranking entities based on business metrics

## 🎯 Final Outcome

* Built a reusable SQL-based analytical framework

## 💡 Key Insights

* Business performance varies significantly across time, customers, and product dimensions
* A small subset of entities often contributes disproportionately to overall performance
* Segmentation and ranking are essential for identifying high-impact areas
* Combining multiple analytical techniques provides a holistic understanding of business performance

---

## 📈 Business Recommendations

* Use time-based analysis to monitor growth trends and detect anomalies early
* Segment customers and products to apply targeted strategies
* Focus on high-performing segments to maximize revenue and efficiency
* Use ranking and performance analysis to prioritize key business drivers
* Implement recurring reporting based on these analytical modules for continuous monitoring
